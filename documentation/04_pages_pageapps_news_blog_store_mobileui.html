Intro

Let's make a small definition of these elements so things are clear and easy to understand 

Pages are classic pages present in all sites, just made to look awesome on your mobile device, they are present on mobile, tablet, desktops, and actually any devices out there

PageApps are mobile styled pages. Most mobile devices / apps use these features to make a page look better. Some of these features are just "tunned" pages and some of them for example tasklists are usually present just on mobile apps. These are easy to use, and give a more native feel to your device. 

News, Store, Blog: These pages are simple page templates, not connected to a CMS, content will be added manually and will be added just like editing the following pages. Duplicating and replacing content with your own. This has a separate section at the end of this documentation

MobileUI: As the name says Mobile User Interfaces. We've created these for customers who plan on converting our products to applications. There is a separate section at the end of the documentation explaing what these are and what license is required if you plan on building a paid application.

 

One more thing that needs to be mentioned.

Some pages are populated with content / features presented in the second and third chapter. Where this happens, we won't be describing how those content features go. So wherever you see <!--Your content features here --> know that that section accepts and can be populated with any content you wish.

As a small rule of thumb, if the item you wish to populate is not fullscreen / fullscreen background / coverpage / image slider it will generally accept all the features. In case they dont or you encounter any bug, please message us and we'll gladly explain if your implementation is or isn't achiavable for that page structure.

PageApps Fullscreen

Fullscreen pages have a different structure than the rest. They're still inside the page-content but afterwards there are some things that need to be mentioned 

<div class="page-fullscreen bg-1">

    </div>

To change the background images open framework.css and CTRL+F or CMD+F for bg-1. You'll find 10 background images there that are inplemented by default. You can replace them with your own.
